What changed:

- Narrowed the Zen Hub launch corridor to private opt-in check-ins, milestone reflections, explicit privacy messaging, export, and deletion flows.
- Removed Zen from default dashboard layouts and tour/default launch surfaces, and tightened copy so Zen is clearly private, non-diagnostic, and excluded from matching, ranking, and org analytics.
- Added lifecycle instrumentation for proof artifacts, proof freshness, verification workflows, reveal workflows, intro expiry/withdrawal, no-shows, overrides, and portfolio publication/indexing state changes.
- Added proof/trust snapshot recomputation and publication-state recomputation hooks, plus admin metrics/dashboard support for proof, trust, workflow, and publication health.
- Added privacy hardening so fairness segmentation uses demographic opt-in data rather than Zen opt-in data, and restored disabled client stubs for deferred self-assessment/work-schedule endpoints.

Why:

- Block 6 requires Zen to stay in a minimal protected MVP corridor while giving product and ops enough durable instrumentation to audit proof, trust, reveal, and portfolio-publication workflows.
- The analytics layer needed first-class proof/trust metrics and lifecycle events without leaking Zen content or turning Zen into a broader measurement surface.
